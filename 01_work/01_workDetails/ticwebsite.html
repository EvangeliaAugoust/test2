Google UX Design Certificate Project 2

TicketFlix Website

Role

UX Researcher, UI/UX Designer

Tool

Figma, Google Doc, Photopea, FFmpeg

Duration

November 2024 to December 2024 - 3 Weeks

Overview

The Product

TicketFlix is a website that lets users browse movies, book tickets, and manage their cinema visits from their desktop. It’s designed to make exploring movies, comparing options, and booking seats easy and hassle-free.



The Goal

❛❛ Making movie ticket booking easy with a user-friendly website.

TicketFlix helps users find movies, pick showtimes, and book tickets effortlessly with a clear and simple interface.

Research

Secondary Research

Through my secondary research, I focused on understanding whether a web-based ticketing system for booking movie tickets would be effective and how it compares to mobile applications. According to a published paper titled 'A Case Study on Online Ticket Booking System Project' it was found that users often prefer a web-based system due to its ease of use and comprehensive features, such as detailed information on movie details, reviews, and other relevant movie details. This makes the booking process more informative and straightforward, as opposed to mobile apps, which some users find complicated and uncomfortable to navigate. In fact, the study suggests that while mobile apps are widely available, many users feel uneasy using them for ticket bookings, further emphasizing the preference for web-based platforms.

Ideating Solutions

How Might We's

By reading more papers like the previous one and turning them into opportunities for design through well-defined questions, I found it easier to create a wider variety of design solutions.

  1. 1. How can we make the website’s layout clearer so users can book tickets faster with fewer clicks?
  2. 2. How can we design a ticket selection and payment process that feels easy and accessible for everyone?
  3. 3. How can we help users quickly find and compare movies, showtimes, and movie theaters without confusion?
Informational Architecture

Sitemap

The sitemap I created structured and categorized the main pages and features that will be available to the user. I wanted the process to be as simple as possible and naturally guide the user to reach their destination with ease. Thus, adding to their user experience and keeping them on their happy path.



User Flows

I created three user flows that features the main functionality that the TicketFlix website would provide the user with.

  1. 1. Booking a movie ticket through the selection and checkout process.
  2. 2. Managing bookings by canceling or rating past movies.
  3. 3. Updating account settings, adding coupons, and removing saved cards.

Digital Wireframes

Usability Study

I then created a moderated usability study script and a clear list of participant criteria in a Google Doc, along with important project details like KPIs and goals.
You can view the full research study plans here.

  1. The prompts I've tasked participating user with are:
  2. 1. From the homepage, use filters to select a cinema and view available film screenings.
  3. 2. Select the upcoming Sunday to see the available movie screenings using filters.
  4. 3. Navigate to the "Comedy" section, filter movies under 12 euros, and select "Mickey 17."
  5. 4. Book two tickets for March 14 at 9:00 PM.
  6. 5. Choose seats in row C.
  7. 6. Finally, confirm your order and complete the payment process, saving your payment card for future use.

Based on participants' feedback from the task prompts, I turned my findings into actionable insights and prioritized them by their impact on the user flow.

  • P0 (Must Have)
    • • It would be helpful to include a set of frequently asked questions (FAQs) at the bottom of each page when the genre changes (e.g., homepage, Adventure section, Drama section, etc.).
    • • When a user navigates to the movie details page, it would be beneficial to display a progress indicator at the top of the page, outlining the four steps to complete the booking: Tickets → Seats → Checkout → Confirmation.

  • P1 (Nice to Have)
    • • Adding a short description under the available movies on each genre-specific page (e.g., homepage, Adventure section, Drama section, etc.) would enhance the browsing experience.


Style Guide

After conducting usability studies and iterating on my digital wireframes, I started thinking more about the typeface, color, and components I will be needing for mockups. Thus, I created a style guide to make the design process more efficient.

High-Fidelity

Reflecting Back

The TicketFlix website makes it easier for users to book movie tickets from any device, giving them a seamless alternative to mobile apps. With a clear layout and smooth navigation, it simplifies the booking process, especially for those who prefer browsing on a larger screen.

This project helped me better understand how to design for different platforms and adapt user flows based on real needs. It also reinforced how crucial usability testing and iteration are for creating a great user experience. Moving forward, further refinements based on user feedback could make the website even more accessible and efficient.
