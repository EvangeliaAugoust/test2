Skyscanner App Redesign

Role

UX/UI Design
Content Strategist

Tool

Figma, Photopea

Duration

February 2025
2 Days

Overview

Preface

Skyscanner is a popular flight search engine that allows users to find and compare airfare across multiple airlines. As someone who frequently uses this app for trip planning, I wanted to explore potential improvements in its user experience and interface, focusing on the journey from the homepage to the flight details page.

❛❛ Skyscanner app is used by millions of user. To redesign something that that is already have a existing user base is itself is a challenge.

Research

With limited time following a typical design process was challenge. When it came to user research I could not do more. But I did talk to some friends and asked their existing experience of using Skyscanner app and their pain-points.

I then began with careful observation of each screen, completing the desired task, going back and forward, taking notes on my initial thoughts like what's missing and what can be done to make user experience more intuitive. My goal was to refine the clarity, hierarchy, and efficiency of the experience while keeping familiar patterns intact.


Problems and Initial Findings

  • Homepage
    • • Lack of a clear Call-to-Action (CTA) for immediate flight searches.
    • • Distracting elements like cards and oversized ads shift focus away from core functionality.

  • Explore Page
    • • Unclear meaning of "Explore Everywhere", leaving users unsure about what they are viewing.

  • Search Page
    • • Dark UI – The search form (departure, destination, dates) is the only visible element, while the rest of the screen remains completely black, making the interface feel unbalanced.

  • Flights Page
    • • Filters are too broad, making it harder to refine searches.
    • • Key flight details are insufficient.

  • Details Page
    • • Booking providers list lacks sorting & filtering, making it difficult to compare options efficiently.
    • • Limited information on booking providers.


UX Principles

Throughout the design process, I applied key UX principles, particularly Fitts's Law and Hick’s Law, to enhance usability and efficiency.

Wireframing

With a focus on improving usability and addressing key issues, I began structuring the app’s information architecture and layout through wireframing. Starting with rough sketches, I iterated on ideas to enhance clarity and efficiency. These wireframes helped visualize essential improvements while maintaining familiar patterns for a seamless user experience.

User Interface
  • Key Improvements
    • • Clearer CTA & streamlined homepag - The flight search form is now prominently placed on the homepage for a more intuitive experience.
    • • Refined Explore section - "Explore Everywhere" has been renamed "Great Flight Deals Worldwide" and now features a carousel, making it easier to browse offers.
    • • Improved filtering & flight details – Filters now include icons, starting prices, and a 'Non-stop only' option for better usability. Each flight listing also displays total travel time for clearer comparisons.
    • • Enhanced booking provider section – Added sorting & filtering options ("Best Reviews", "Cheapest"), along with additional details for each provider, such as customer support availability and baggage policies.

For example, in the existing app, the homepage lacked a clear Call-to-Action (CTA) for immediate flight searches. Users had to click on the 'Flights' button just to access the search form, as shown in the picture below. By placing the flight search directly on the homepage, I introduced a clear CTA and streamlined the booking flow, making the process faster and more intuitive.

Conclusion

The TicketFlix website extends the platform’s accessibility, allowing users to book movie tickets effortlessly from any device. By focusing on an intuitive layout and seamless navigation, it enhances the online booking experience and provides an alternative to mobile apps for those who prefer browsing on a larger screen.

Throughout this project, I deepened my understanding of designing for different platforms and the importance of adapting user flows based on user preferences. This experience reinforced the significance of usability testing and iterative design in crafting an efficient and enjoyable digital experience. Moving forward, further refinements based on additional user feedback could further enhance the website’s functionality and accessibility.
