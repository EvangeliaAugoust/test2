Google UX Design Certificate Project 1

TicketFlix Mobile App

Role

UX Designer

Tool

Figma, Google Doc, Google Slide

Duration

October 2024 to November 2024 - 3 Weeks

Overview

The Product

TicketFlix is an app that simplifies the process of purchasing movie tickets by allowing users to discover movies playing near them, book seats, and effortlessly manage their bookings. The typical user ranges from 16-45 years old, primarily targeting movie enthusiasts looking for a quick and practical way to organize their cinema experience.



Problem

Movie enthusiasts often struggle with finding reliable showtime information and navigating complex booking platforms. Many existing solutions lack personalization, making the process frustrating and discouraging for users seeking a seamless movie-going experience.



The Goal

Simplifying the movie-going experience by providing an efficient and user-friendly mobile ticketing platform. Empowering users to effortlessly discover movies, book tickets, and manage their bookings while delivering a seamless integration with their lifestyle and preferences.

Empathize and Research

After conducting foundational research, I developed two user personas based on Google's provided examples, adapting them to align with the needs and goals of my project. These personas were crafted to represent the target audience, highlighting their motivations, pain points, and behaviors to guide the design process effectively.

Usability Study
Usability Study - Round 1 Findings

I've written an usability study moderated interview script and recorded the characteristics a participant should have in a cohesive google doc, among other factors that played into the creation of this project including KPI's and project goals.
You can view the full research study plans here.

  1. The prompts I've tasked participating user with are:
  2. 1. From the home screen, create a profile.
  3. 2. Choose Thessaloniki to locate available films in cinemas.
  4. 3. Select a cinema to see the available film screenings.
  5. 4. Select the available movies for the upcoming weekend and choose the first movie.
  6. 5. Book 2 tickets for March 14 at 9 pm.
  7. 6. Choose seats in row C.
  8. 7. Finally, confirm your order and complete the payment process, saving your payment card for future use.

After conducting the usability study, where participants were asked to complete the tasks outlined in the prompts, I documented their responses in a structured format. You can view the detailed results for each prompt here.
Based on these responses, I synthesized my findings into actionable insights and prioritized them based on how negatively they impact the users' flow.

  • P0 (Must Have)
    • • The filters on the home screen are too small, making them difficult to read and interact with. Their size and font should be increased for better visibility. Additionally, selected and unselected filters should be more distinguishable.
    • • Instead of placing the QR code on the confirmation screen, it may be more beneficial to add it to the "Orders → Upcoming" section. Once the booking is complete, a notification could alert users that their new ticket is now available there.

  • P1 (Nice to Have)
    • • The confirmation screen could include an "Add to Google Wallet / Apple Wallet" button alongside the existing "Download Ticket" option for a smoother experience.



Style Guide

After revising my previous designs to address these issues, I could begin planning out color, font, and style for the mockups. High-Fidelity

Mockups and High-Fidelity Prototype

During the hi-fi prototyping phase, I concentrated on refining the visual design and interactions, ensuring a polished and seamless user experience while addressing any remaining usability issues.



Usability Study - Round 2 Findings

I synthesized the newly collected data from the high-fidelity usability study into actionable insights and prioritized the most critical issues based on the participants' responses, which are documented in this spreadsheet.


  • P0 (Must Have)
    • • On the Location Page, users should be able to zoom in and select a city directly from the map for a more intuitive selection process.
    • • On the Movie Details Page, it should be clear which cinema is screening the selected movie, so users can easily identify where they can watch it.

  • P1 (Nice to Have)
    • • On the Select Location Page, adding an "or" between the "Enable Location" and "Select a City" buttons, highlighted in yellow, could make it clearer that users have two distinct ways to choose their location.

Reflecting Back

TicketFlix has the potential to transform the way people plan their movie outings. By simplifying the ticket booking process and creating a user-friendly experience, it can make cinema visits more accessible and enjoyable.

Overall, I've learned so much from this project, having the Google's UX Design Certificate Courses guide me through each step of the process. I’ve gained valuable insights into the importance of user feedback and usability testing. These processes are crucial for identifying pain points, making them indispensable tools in creating a user-centered experience. After several more usability test studies and finalizing design details and more, I would potentially look into seeing if this is a viably publishable app.
